# Issues and solutions

> Government publishes a problem; organisations propose; one solution wins.

## Overview

Issues and solutions is the centre of Ecowise's collaboration loop. A government user
publishes an issue describing a sustainability challenge. Organisations propose solutions.
Individuals upvote the solutions they find compelling and register interest in contributing.
The issue owner accepts a single winning solution, and the accepting organisation can then
promote that solution into a full project — taking the platform's planning, matching, and
pledge surfaces with it.

This is the route from "we have a problem" to "we have a project being delivered."

## What it does

- Government creates, edits, and removes issues.
- Organisations submit solutions to open issues.
- Individuals upvote solutions and register contributor interest.
- Government accepts the winning solution.
- The winner's organisation promotes the accepted solution into a project.

## Who uses it

Governments publish; organisations propose; individuals influence and join.

## Key features

- Full lifecycle in one module — no jumping between disjoint surfaces.
- Acceptance is reversible until promotion.
- Promotion creates a project with the solution copy carried forward.
